Double Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

These cookies are for hardcore chocolate lovers. We use an double dose of cocoa powder that keeps the texture firm and photo-worthy, while simultaneously delivering that warm dopamine rush you need.

The recipe is simple, and chopped nuts are an additional option. Just try to leave enough cookie dough to actually bake with!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (125g)
  • 2/3 cup cocoa powder (65g)
  • 3/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 heaping tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened (113g)
  • 3/4 cup white or brown sugar (161g)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • (optional) 1/4 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Sift dry ingredients in a small bowl, and set aside.
  3. Cream butter and sugar together for 2-3 minutes, then add egg and vanilla and continue mixing for another minute.
  4. Using a wooden spoon, add dry ingredients to wet. The mixture will be extremely thick and a spatula will not work!
  5. Mix in chocolate chips, saving a handful for the top, and walnuts.
  6. Drop onto parchment-lined cookie sheet using a spoon, as close as you like because they will not spread. Push the leftover chocolate chips onto the tops of the cookies.
  7. Bake for 10 min, then remove from oven and let cool for a few minutes on pan. They will not appear done, but trust the process!
  8. Move cookies to cooling rack using a spatula, and enjoy! Can be stored on the shelf in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
