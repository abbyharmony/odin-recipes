Fresh Basil Pesto

Description

Homemade pesto is about as simple as it gets, and is a great recipe for gardeners with too much basil on their hands. Feel free to sub half spinach or radish greens, too.

Although we mellow the garlic by pre-roasting, feel free to throw in some raw cloves if you want to ward off vampires!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves, packed
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated parmesan or romano cheese
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts or chopped walnuts
  • 3-5 cloves roasted garlic (see below)
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Roast garlic by cutting the top off a bulb to expose the cloves, drizzle with olive oil, wrap in foil and bake at 400 degrees F for 30 min.
  2. Allow the garlic to cool, then remove 3-5 cloves from their peels for the pesto. Feel free to spread remaining cloves on bread, mashed potatoes, or pasta!
  3. Place the basil and nuts into a food processor, and pulse several times.
  4. Add the garlic and cheese, and pulse a few more times. Scrape the sides down using a spatula.
  5. While processor is running, slowly pour in olive oil until incorporated. Or if using a closed machine, add oil and then run.
  6. Scrape down sides, add salt and pepper to taste, and pulse a few last times.
  7. Serve over pasta, bread, and more! Store in an airtight container covering surface with plastic wrap, and keep in refrigerator up to one week.
